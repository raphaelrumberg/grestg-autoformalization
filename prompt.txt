## Legal logic

### Z 1 - Change in shareholder structure
Tax is triggered when, within seven years, at least 75% of the shares in a property-owning entity pass directly to new shareholders. For each acquirer-type node individually, sum all direct edges from that acquirer to target_entity. If any single acquirer's direct total is >= 75.0, Z 1 is triggered and that acquirer is the taxpayer.

### Z 2 - Share consolidation (applies only if Z 1 does not)
Tax is triggered when at least 75% of shares in the property-owning entity are consolidated in the hand of a single acquirer.

For each acquirer, compute their total effective stake in target_entity by summing the values of all distinct paths from that acquirer to target_entity. Each path's value is the product of the ownership percentages along its edges. A direct edge counts as a one-hop path.

For example, if A holds 80% of B, and B holds 90% of PropCo, then A's stake via that path is 80% × 90% = 72%. If A also holds 60% of C, and C holds 20% of PropCo, A's stake via that second path is 60% × 20% = 12%. A's total effective stake is 72% + 12% = 84%.

Tax is triggered if any single acquirer's total effective stake >= 75.0. That acquirer is the taxpayer.

### Priority rule
Z 1 is evaluated first. Z 2 is only evaluated if Z 1 is not triggered.
